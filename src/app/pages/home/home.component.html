what are the implications of regulated online casinos?

Before November 2014, remote operators that provided gambling services to UK players tended to be licensed and regulated in the European Union, with Gibraltar and Malta particularly popular bases. Less than 15% of these were licensed by the UK Gambling Commission.

However, when the Gambling (Licensing & Advertising) Act 2014 entered force, it required all offshore operators to each hold a licence from the UK Gambling Commission. This change was aimed at further protecting UK players by making the regulation of these companies consistent.

Each of these gambling brands must adhere to particular, strict standards in order to both obtain and keep its UKGC license. These technical standards include testing its Random Number Generator software to verify that its games and payouts are fair.

Operators also need to clearly inform customers about how their funds are being protected and comply with policies and practices aimed at tackling money-laundering. They must also exercise the social responsibility of preventing minors from gambling and act to prevent compulsive gambling.
